Automation specification framework

They specifically asked for this format — know it cold

The framework they use

When specifying an automation, always define these 5 things:

Trigger
What event starts the automation? (e.g. "When a new client signs the contract")
Condition
What must be true for the automation to run? (e.g. "Only if client type is Enterprise")
Action
What happens automatically? (e.g. "Create folder in Drive, send welcome email, add to ClickUp")
Approval
Does a human need to approve anything? (e.g. "Manager approves before contract is sent")
Exception
What happens if something goes wrong? (e.g. "If email fails, flag in Slack within 1 hour")
Remember: most automation problems are process problems in disguise. Fix the process first, then automate it.

Likely questions & how to answer

Things to avoid saying

Avoid

"I am a fast learner."

Everyone says this. Replace with a specific example — "When I joined Myridius I had to learn the full Zeus codebase and client requirements in 2 weeks. Within a month I was running discovery sessions solo."

Avoid

"I am good at supporting the team."

This sounds passive. Say: "I take ownership of specific deliverables and drive them to completion — I do not wait to be told what to do next."

Avoid

Long answers without a point.

Give your answer in 2-3 sentences first. Pause. If they want more they will ask. Rambling signals poor communication skills — a non-negotiable for this role.

Avoid

"I do not have experience with that."

Say instead: "I have not used that specific tool, but I learn systems fast. I focus on the business logic first, then improve how the tool is used around it."

Best questions to ask them

Question 1

"What does a successful first 30 days look like for this role?"

Shows you want to deliver fast, not just get comfortable.

Question 2

"What is one implementation pain point your team is facing right now?"

Shows you are already thinking like a solutions consultant, not a job applicant.

Question 3

"How does your team currently handle requirements gathering with clients?"

Shows you understand the core of the role and want to know where you can improve their current process.
